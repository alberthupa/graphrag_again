Customer lifetime value: The total amount of money a customer is expected to spend on a business's products or services over the course of their relationship with the business. This can be calculated using a variety of methods, such as the customer acquisition cost method (which divides the customer lifetime value by the customer acquisition cost) or the discounted cash flow method (which takes into account the time value of money). This is calculated by dividing the net profit by the cost of the investment and multiplying by 100.Formula: ROI = (net profit / cost of investment) x 100 Cost per acquisition (CPA): The cost of acquiring a new customer, calculated by dividing the total marketing and sales expenses by the number of new customers acquired.Formula: CPA = total marketing and sales expenses / number of new customers Customer acquisition cost (CAC): The total cost of acquiring a customer, including marketing, sales, and product development expenses.
