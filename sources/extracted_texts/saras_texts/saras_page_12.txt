FAQ What are eCommerce KPIs and why are they important? eCommerce KPIs are key performance indicators that measure and track the performance of an online store. They are important because they help eCommerce businesses understand their strengths and weaknesses, optimize their operations, improve customer satisfaction, and increase sales and profitability. What are some examples of eCommerce KPIs and how are they calculated? There are many different eCommerce KPIs that can be grouped by different business functions, such as marketing, sales, operations, finance, and customer service. Some examples are: - Conversion rate: The percentage of website visitors who complete a desired action, such as making a purchase or signing up for a newsletter. It is calculated by dividing the number of conversions by the number of visitors. - Return on ad spend (ROAS): The amount of revenue generated by a specific advertising campaign or channel. It is calculated by dividing the revenue attributed to ads by the total advertising cost. - Customer lifetime value (CLTV): The estimated total amount of revenue that a customer will generate for a business over their entire relationship. It is calculated by multiplying the average order value by the average number of orders per customer by the average retention rate. - Inventory turnover rate: The number of times that a business sells and replaces its inventory in a given period. It is calculated by dividing the cost of goods sold by the average inventory value.
